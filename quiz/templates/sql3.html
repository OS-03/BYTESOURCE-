SQL operators are symbols that allow you to perform operations on one or more operands to produce a result. These operators are used in SQL queries to manipulate data in databases.

Types of SQL Operators

There are several types of SQL operators, including arithmetic operators, comparison operators, logical operators, and bitwise operators.

1. Arithmetic Operators

Arithmetic operators are used to perform mathematical operations on numeric operands. They include addition (+), subtraction (-), multiplication (*), division (/), and modulus (%).

2. Comparison Operators

Comparison operators are used to compare values in SQL queries. They include equal to (=), not equal to (!=), greater than (>), less than (<), greater than or equal to (>=), and less than or equal to (<=).

3. Logical Operators

Logical operators are used to combine multiple conditions in SQL queries. They include AND, OR, and NOT.

4. Bitwise Operators

Bitwise operators are used to perform operations at the bit level. They include AND (&), OR (|), XOR (^), NOT (~), and bit shift operators (<< and >>).

Examples

Here are some examples of using SQL operators in queries:

Arithmetic Operator Example: SELECT salary * 1.1 AS increased_salary FROM employees;

Comparison Operator Example: SELECT * FROM products WHERE price > 100;

Logical Operator Example: SELECT * FROM customers WHERE age > 18 AND city = 'New York';
